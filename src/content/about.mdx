## Background

I'm Aaron — a scientist turned healthcare strategist based in Washington, DC. I have a PhD in biology and neuroscience, and I've spent the years since working at the intersection of life science, clinical evidence, and health systems strategy.

My current work is in healthcare consulting, where I help organizations think through problems at the strategic and analytical layer. In parallel, I spend a lot of time on AI and machine learning — specifically how these tools are beginning to reshape drug discovery, clinical research, and the broader practice of medicine.

## What I write about

This site is where I work through ideas I find genuinely interesting. Posts tend to cover:

- **AI and ML in biotech** — what's working, what's overhyped, and where the field is actually headed
- **Drug discovery and neuroscience** — mechanistic thinking about how we understand and treat disease
- **Healthcare strategy** — how health systems, payers, and life science companies make decisions
- **Data and tooling** — practical notes on working with biological and clinical data

## This site

Less a resume than a working notebook. A place to write things down, develop ideas over time, and keep a record of projects and observations that would otherwise disappear.
